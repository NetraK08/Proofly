How it works

One pipeline, four checkpoints.

Every claim moves through the same disciplined sequence — so two reviewers, or two countries, never disagree on the same evidence.

01 · read

Parse the conversation

Proofly pulls the actual damage claim out of the chat transcript, ignoring small talk and noting the object, part, and issue the user described.

02 · inspect

Score the images

Each photo is checked against the object's minimum evidence checklist — angle, lighting, framing — before any verdict is allowed.

03 · cross-check

Compare claim vs. pixels

The described damage is matched against what's actually visible. Mismatches, missing context, or suspicious edits get flagged immediately.

04 · decide

Weigh history, then rule

Past claim patterns add context — never override clear images — and Proofly outputs a status, severity, and a justification anyone can audit.

Built for review teams

What ships in every decision.

Not just a yes or no — Proofly hands reviewers everything they'd need to defend the call.

Grounded justifications

Every verdict cites the specific image IDs and visual evidence behind it — no black-box scoring.

Evidence-standard checks

Each object and issue family has a minimum photo checklist. Claims that fall short are routed to review, not guessed.

Authenticity flags

Detects mismatched objects, cropped or obstructed shots, and signs of manipulated or reused images before they reach a verdict.

History-aware, not history-led

Past claim patterns add risk context in the margins — they never override what the photos clearly show.

Severity at a glance

Each supported claim is scored none through high, so triage and payout teams can prioritize without re-reading the case.

Audit-ready exports

Every decision lands as a clean, structured row — ready for spreadsheets, dashboards, or downstream claims systems.

Proofly doesn't just say yes — it says why not, too.

When evidence falls short, Proofly raises the exact reason instead of a generic rejection — so claimants know precisely what to resubmit.
